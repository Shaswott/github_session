10 Places to Visit in Kathmandu,Nepal

  • Swayambhunath Stupa

    Swayambhunath Stupa, also known as the Monkey Temple, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most sacred Buddhist sites in Nepal. It is located on a hilltop overlooking Kathmandu and offers stunning views of the city. The stupa is surrounded by prayer flags and hundreds of monkeys that roam freely around the area.

  • Pashupatinath Temple

    Pashupatinath Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Nepal. It is located on the banks of the Bagmati River and is known for its stunning architecture and intricate carvings. The temple is also home to many sadhus, or holy men, who come to meditate and seek blessings.

  • Monkey Temple

    The Monkey Temple is another name for Swayambhunath Stupa, which is known for its resident monkeys. The temple is located on a hilltop and offers stunning views of Kathmandu. It is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike.

  • Boudha Stupa

    Boudha Stupa is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest stupas in the world. It is located in the town of Boudha and is a popular destination for both tourists and locals. The stupa is surrounded by shops and restaurants and is a great place to experience the local culture.

  • Basantapur Durbar Square

    Basantapur Durbar Square is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the historic seat of Nepalese royalty. It is located in the heart of Kathmandu and is home to many temples, palaces, and courtyards. The square is a great place to experience the local culture and architecture.

  • Bhaktapur Durbar Square

    Bhaktapur Durbar Square is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a well-preserved example of Newar architecture. It is located in the town of Bhaktapur and is home to many temples, palaces, and courtyards. The square is a great place to experience the local culture and architecture.

  • Patan Durbar Square
